# Pitch Deck Outline

## Slide 1

EdgeVector

Own your data. Share it on your terms.

---

## Slide 2

Problem

Your data is scattered across platforms that own it, sell it, and lock you in.

* documents on Google Drive
* notes on Notion
* photos on iCloud
* messages on Slack

You have **no control over who accesses it or how it's used.**

---

## Slide 3

Solution

FoldDB

A personal database where **all access is mediated by policy-enforcing interfaces.** Your node, your keys, your rules.

---

## Slide 4

How It Works

1. Install FoldDB on your device
2. Ingest your files — they stay local, encrypted
3. Share on your terms — set who can access what, and how

---

## Slide 5

Example

A health app wants to read your medication list.

1. App requests access via Exemem
2. You see a consent screen: "App X wants to read your medications"
3. You approve with your passkey
4. Your node decrypts and returns only the authorized data
5. You revoke access any time — takes effect instantly

---

## Slide 6

Key Principles

* Data ownership by default
* End-to-end encryption — no platform sees plaintext
* Permissioned sharing on your terms
* Open-source infrastructure

---

## Slide 7

Architecture

```
Third-Party App → Exemem (encrypted relay) → User's Node (decryption + access control)
```

Exemem never sees plaintext. It's a dumb relay with a clipboard.

---

## Slide 8

Why Now

Three converging trends:

1. Privacy regulation accelerating (GDPR, state laws)
2. Platform trust eroding (data breaches, policy changes)
3. Cryptographic tooling mature enough for real-world use

---

## Slide 9

Long-Term Vision

A **network of sovereign data nodes** where sharing creates value without surrendering control.
